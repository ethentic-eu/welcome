# Welcome to the ethentic<sup>&reg;</sup> Space!

This is the place, where everything becomes **e**lectronic while remaining au**thentic**. The technical foundation 
for realising this [vision](https://www.ethentic.eu/#about) are the **ethentic<sup>&reg;** *Building Blocks*, 
which encapsulate the technical functionality in a reusable manner to ease the creation of tailormade **ethentic<sup>&reg;** *Solutions* 
and **ethentic<sup>&reg;** *Services* based on these foundational technical components.

<center><img height="800" src="https://github.com/ethentic-eu/welcome/blob/main/img/ethentic-Building-Blocks.svg"/></center>


## ethentic<sup>&reg;</sup> Authorizer

The **ethentic<sup>&reg;** **Authorizer** is a trustworthy building block, which allows to *identify* and 
*authenticate* users and services and *authorize* corresponding requests based on a variety of standards 
including [OAuth 2.0](https://datatracker.ietf.org/wg/oauth/about/) according to widely accepted international 
standard (see [RFC 6749](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc6749.html), 
[RFC 6750](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc6750.html), [RFC 7009](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc7009.html),
[RFC 7521](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc7521.html), [RFC 7523](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc7523.html), 
[RFC 7591](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc7591.html), [RFC 7592](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc7592.html),
[RFC 7636](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc7636.html), [RFC 7662](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc7662.html),
[RFC 8414](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc8414.html), [RFC 8628](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc8628.html),
[RFC 9101](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc9101.html), [RFC 9207](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc9207.html) 
and [RFC 9449](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc9449.html)) and other relevant standards including 
[OpenID Connect](http://openid.net/specs/openid-connect-core-1_0.html), 
[Security Assertion Markup Language (SAML)](https://www.oasis-open.org/standard/saml/), [User Managed Access (UMA)](https://docs.kantarainitiative.org/uma/wg/rec-oauth-uma-grant-2.0.html) and [W3C WebAuthn](https://www.w3.org/TR/webauthn-2/) for example.

## ethentic<sup>&reg;</sup> Bridge

The **ethentic<sup>&reg;** **Bridge** is a highly flexible framework and gateway for the easy and trustworthy integration 
of *authentic sources* using standardised interfaces according to [ETSI TS 119 478](https://www.etsi.org/deliver/etsi_ts/119400_119499/119478/01.01.01_60/ts_119478v010101p.pdf)
for example.

## ethentic<sup>&reg;</sup> Issuer

The **ethentic<sup>&reg;** **Issuer** can be used to *issue* verifiable credentials and electronic attestations of attributes
in standardised *formats*, such as [SD-JWT VC](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/draft-ietf-oauth-sd-jwt-vc/),
[ISO/IEC 18013-5](https://www.iso.org/obp/ui/en/#iso:std:iso-iec:18013:-5:ed-1:v1:en) and
[W3C VCDM 2.0](https://www.w3.org/TR/vc-data-model-2.0/), using standardised *protocols* like
[OID4VCI](https://openid.net/specs/openid-4-verifiable-credential-issuance-1_0.html) with relevant
*profiles*, such as [HAIP](https://openid.net/specs/openid4vc-high-assurance-interoperability-profile-1_0-final.html), 
[ETSI TS 119 472-1](https://www.etsi.org/deliver/etsi_ts/119400_119499/11947201/01.02.01_60/ts_11947201v010201p.pdf) and 
[ETSI TS 119 472-3](https://portal.etsi.org/webapp/WorkProgram/Report_WorkItem.asp?WKI_ID=74935&curItemNr=4&totalNrItems=8&optDisplay=10&titleType=all&qSORT=HIGHVERSION&qETSI_ALL=&SearchPage=TRUE&qWKI_REFERENCE=19472&qINCLUDE_SUB_TB=True&qINCLUDE_MOVED_ON=&qSTOP_FLG=N&qKEYWORD_BOOLEAN=OR&qCLUSTER_BOOLEAN=OR&qFREQUENCIES_BOOLEAN=OR&qSTOPPING_OUTDATED=&butExpertSearch=Search&includeNonActiveTB=FALSE&includeSubProjectCode=FALSE&qREPORT_TYPE=SUMMARY) for example.

## ethentic<sup>&reg;</sup> SDK

The **ethentic<sup>&reg;** **SDK** is a comprehensive platform independent *Software Development Kit* (SDK), which
makes it easy to create wallets for natural and legal persons, such as [European Digital Identity Wallets](https://ec.europa.eu/digital-building-blocks/sites/spaces/EUDIGITALIDENTITYWALLET/pages/694487738/EU+Digital+Identity+Wallet+Home)
or [European Business Wallets](https://github.com/eu-business-wallet) for example, or similar tailor-made applications on various *platforms*
including Android, iOS, Windows, macOS and Linux for example. It provides a comprehensive and flexible framework
for the secure storage and application of *cryptographic keys* in trusted execution environments, smart cards
and other cryptographic hardware devices and supports the issuing, requesting, obtaining, selecting, combining, storing, deleting, sharing and presenting of verifiable credentials
and *electronic attestations of attributes* as well as the creation and validation of electronic *signatures, seals* and *time stamps*.

## ethentic<sup>&reg;</sup> Signer

The **ethentic<sup>&reg;** **Signer** can be used to *create* advanced and qualified electronic
*signatures* and *seals* in relevant digital signature formats, such as
*CAdES* ([ETSI EN 319 122-1](https://www.etsi.org/deliver/etsi_en/319100_319199/31912201/01.03.01_60/en_31912201v010301p.pdf)),
*PAdES* ([ETSI EN 319 142-1](https://www.etsi.org/deliver/etsi_en/319100_319199/31914201/01.02.01_60/en_31914201v010201p.pdf)),
*XAdES* ([ETSI EN 319 132-1](https://www.etsi.org/deliver/etsi_en/319100_319199/31913201/01.03.01_60/en_31913201v010301p.pdf)),
*JAdES* ([ETSI TS 119 182](https://www.etsi.org/deliver/etsi_ts/119100_119199/11918201/01.02.01_60/ts_11918201v010201p.pdf)) and soon also
*CB-AdES* ([ETSI TS 119 152-1](https://portal.etsi.org/webapp/WorkProgram/Report_WorkItem.asp?WKI_ID=63599&curItemNr=2&totalNrItems=3&optDisplay=10&titleType=all&qSORT=HIGHVERSION&qETSI_ALL=&SearchPage=TRUE&qINCLUDE_SUB_TB=True&qINCLUDE_MOVED_ON=&qSTOP_FLG=N&qKEYWORD_BOOLEAN=OR&qCLUSTER_BOOLEAN=OR&qFREQUENCIES_BOOLEAN=OR&qTITLE=CB%2DAdES&qSTOPPING_OUTDATED=&butExpertSearch=Search&includeNonActiveTB=FALSE&includeSubProjectCode=FALSE&qREPORT_TYPE=SUMMARY)),
based on standardised *procedures* such as [ETSI 319 102-1](https://www.etsi.org/deliver/etsi_en/319100_319199/31910201/01.04.01_60/en_31910201v010401p.pdf) 
for example.

*PAdES* ([ETSI EN 319 142-1](https://www.etsi.org/deliver/etsi_en/319100_319199/31914201/01.02.01_60/en_31914201v010201p.pdf)),

## ethentic<sup>&reg;</sup> Verifier

The **ethentic<sup>&reg;** **Verifier** can be used to request and *verify* verifiable credentials and electronic attestations of attributes in 
standardised *formats*, such as [SD-JWT VC](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/draft-ietf-oauth-sd-jwt-vc/), 
[ISO/IEC 18013-5](https://www.iso.org/obp/ui/en/#iso:std:iso-iec:18013:-5:ed-1:v1:en) and 
[W3C VCDM 2.0](https://www.w3.org/TR/vc-data-model-2.0/), using standardised *protocols* like 
[OID4VP](https://openid.net/specs/openid-4-verifiable-presentations-1_0.html) with relevant *profiles*
such as [HAIP](https://openid.net/specs/openid4vc-high-assurance-interoperability-profile-1_0-final.html),
[ETSI TS 119 472-1](https://www.etsi.org/deliver/etsi_ts/119400_119499/11947201/01.02.01_60/ts_11947201v010201p.pdf) and
[ETSI TS 119 472-2](https://www.etsi.org/deliver/etsi_ts/119400_119499/11947202/01.01.01_60/ts_11947202v010101p.pdf), 
with *trust anchors* according to [ETSI TS 119 612](https://www.etsi.org/deliver/etsi_ts/119600_119699/119612/02.04.01_60/ts_119612v020401p.pdf) 
and [ETSI TS 119 602](https://www.etsi.org/deliver/etsi_ts/119600_119699/119602/01.01.01_60/ts_119602v010101p.pdf) for example.
